About this Project

Purpose

This project began with a user centered question: Why is it still so hard for students to find campus events, clubs, and opportunities? Information is often scattered shared through posters, emails, or group chats and itâ€™s easy to miss out. To solve this, I applied a UX first mindset: identify the user pain points, streamline access to information, and reduce friction in discovering student life. The result is a centralized, intuitive platform designed to connect students more easily with their campus and students communities.

Vision

To design a digital hub that can be continuesly improved to meet student needs and the student experience through thoughtful UX, accessibility, and clear, usable information architecture. Ultimately, this webstie aims to make campus involvement feel effortless and inclusive.

Why I Built This

This project gave me the opportunity to combine my experience and technical development, UX research, wireframing, and iterative design. I conducted simple UX research, sketched low-fidelity, and refined the interface to match real user needs. The experience helped me think like a user, designer, not just a developer. Itâ€™s a project grounded in empathy, usability, and real world relevance.

Website Goals

  • Promote student engagement in clubs and activities
  • Make event information simple and quick to find
  • Encourage collaboration across different fields of study
  • Build a stronger, more connected campus community
  • Provide a platform that can grow and evolve with student needs
